MSNBC On SA. - Zifei 9:35pm CST, 11-24-98
Here's what the MSNBC cable news network has to say about Sonic Adventure after they saw it:

According to the specifications Sega released earlier this year, Dreamcast’s graphic engine can generate 3 million polygons per second. At no time has this power been more apparent than when playing “Sonic.” While the environments in “Sonic Adventure” are not photorealistic, the objects within them are crisp, three-dimensional and very clear. The levels shown during the demonstration included a 2-D level in which Sonic, Sega’s blue hedgehog-in-tennis-shoes mascot, runs through a city at night, a level in which Sonic’s pal Knuckles flies around in a three-dimensional canyon, and a level in which another Sonic-buddy runs through a factory. It’s difficult to judge a game without playing it, but at this demonstration the only technological glitch was some pop-in. (Pop-in is a name given to background objects that seem to appear out of nowhere. On racing games, for instance, the scenery along the track may appear on the screen as you drive past it. This is generally the result of the processor’s being overwhelmed by the amount of data it is handling. The Saturn was notorious for pop-in problems.) Considering the amount of detailed graphics being rendered in the game, the speed of the game and the fact that it is still unfinished, some pop-in should be expected.

I'm guessing that they saw an earlier version of the game. Because latest reports out of Japan are saying Sonic Adventure's rid of almost all the pop-ups. And photorealistic imagery is impossible with polygons, so I don't know why they brought that up. As for a 2D level...interesting. Could the Sonic Team made 2D levels of all the 3D levels? Or is the MSNBC reporter reporting the wrong stuff here?

Sonic R PC...First Contact. - Zifei 7:50pm CST, 11-20-98
Or rather, first impression. This is what SegaNet has to say after touching sonic R PC for the first time:

Sega's PC division trots out their excellent Saturn emulator for another go in Sonic R for Windows compatible PCs. The game is a one to one conversion of last year's Saturn title, save for a few minor and hardly noticeable additions. First up to the plate is the game's Direct 3D support; any decent 3D video card will run Sonic R as fast the Saturn version with good texture quality and at a crystal-clear 640 x 480 resolution. The question does rise, why did Sega limit Sonic R's resolution to 640 x 480? Any second-generation 3D card could probably have this puppy racing at 1024 x 768, which would have made Sonic R that much more of a visual treat. Those looking for improved texture detail (i.e., fuller character models) will be disappointed; the polygons may look sharper than on Saturn, but it doesn't look like there's more of them.

Graphics aside, Sonic R PC is a nice little console racer that will have a tough time finding a niche for itself in the competitive PC market. Sonic holds no pull in the land of Quake, and the fruity vocals, which add so much to Sonic R's "cute" value, may painfully detract from this game's marketability to PC gamers. Sega will never succeed in the PC market if it continues to port over year old Saturn games, adding D3D support, some network options, and a pretty box. Look for a full review next week - I'll tell you in great details all that's good and all that's bad in Sonic R PC.

Shigeru Miyamoto On SA. - Zifei 11:07am CST, 11-14-98
Shigeru Miyamoto, the mastermind behind Mario, Zelda, Star Fox, Donkey Kong, Yoshi, and a bunch of others, recently commented on Sonic Adventure. Here's a quote from Next-Generation:

Of course, Sega has its own big game coming soon with its famous mascot Sonic, and we asked Miyamoto-san what he thought of Sonic Adventures. He said that he had not gotten a chance to play the game, but had seen all the movies and read about it. "Does the world really need another Mario-like game?" he asked rhetorically. He expressed some regret that the game didn't appear to have much innovation and said that while it looks like it will be a good game, he had hoped that the crew at Sega would have created a game with more creative oomph. However, he also commented that he has a great deal of respect for the people on the Sonic team, and wants to see the final game.

I for one totally disagrees with what he said. First of all, Sonic Adventure isn't a "Mario-like" game. It's too fast and big to be considered a Mario-type. As for innovations, there's the speed, the size, the graphics, the VMS, the A-life, the character moves, the morphing Perfect Chaos, the RPG elements...need I say more? With all the talk about the up coming Zelda: TooT being the best game of the century, I think Sonic Adventure is the most underrated game of the century.

Sonic R PC. - Zifei 3:20pm CST, 11-11-98
Sonic R PC was released yesterday, here's Sega's official press release:

REDWOOD CITY, Calif.--Nov. 11, 1998--Sega(R) PC released "Sonic R(TM)" today, bringing Sonic the Hedgehog(TM) to the PC in an all-new 3D racing game with a look and feel unlike anything Sonic fans have ever seen. In "Sonic R," gamers take a 3D thrill ride as they race through outer space, an ancient city and a tropical island. Gamers can go off the beaten path to investigate these worlds and uncover hidden power-ups and other surprises along the way.

In "Sonic R" gamers don't have to run this race alone. Now they can race head-to-head with up to four players through a split-screen on one PC, adding a new element of fun and replay value. "Sonic R" is easy enough for the novice to pick-up and play, but full of challenges that'll test the expert gamer's racing skills. "Sonic R" is available in stores nationwide starting this week.

In "Sonic R," gamers can race as Sonic or one of his three pals -- Tails, Knuckles or Amy -- with a goal of collecting gold rings, finding the stolen Chaos Emeralds and making it to the finish line. Gamers can race over the sun-kissed beaches of Resort Island, cruise the streets of the colorful metropolis Radical City, explore the Egyptian maze of the Regal Ruin, speed through "loop 'd loops" and over potholes in the Reactive Factory and soar through space on a hidden track, the Radiant Emerald. Just to keep racers on their toes, random weather effects such as snow and rain cause water to freeze and roads to become slick, adding a new element of challenge to the game.

Gamers are constantly rewarded in "Sonic R" with various hidden elements and power-ups to find along the way. As you race through the levels, you can unlock hidden opponents such as Dr. Robotnik, Metal Robotnik, Super Sonic, Metal Sonic, Teddybear Tails and Mecha Knuckles. To earn these hidden characters, gamers have to be faster than speeding bullets and need to know the short cuts to leave the competition in the dust. Power-ups such as floating stars, spinning shoes, magnetic power-ups or Sonic tokens can give players a winning edge and allow them to get bonus rings, run on water or pick-up relentless speed.

Now don't get to overly excited about this game unless you can't find the Saturn version or don't have a Saturn. This PC game is full of pop-ups and break-ups and is no where near the graphics of the console version...but the speed is still there, just with a lot more slow downs depending on your CPU.
